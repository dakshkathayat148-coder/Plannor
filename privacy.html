Plannor — Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Plannor ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you use the Plannor platform and its integrations, including the ChatGPT / MCP connector, Google Calendar, and accounting integrations (Xero, QuickBooks, MYOB). We comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

Last updated: May 2026

1. Who We Are

Plannor is a multi-tenant care-operations SaaS platform built for NDIS service providers operating in Australia. The platform operator is responsible for the personal information collected through the Plannor web application, mobile interface, and AI connectors including the ChatGPT / MCP integration.

Privacy enquiries: privacy@plannor.com.au  |  Postal: Plannor, Australia

2. Personal Information We Collect

We collect personal information that is reasonably necessary to operate the platform and its connectors. This includes:

  • Account and identity information — name, email address, role, and employment relationship within your organisation.
  • Tenant and organisational context — the registered NDIS provider organisation you are linked to.
  • Operational records — shift schedules, check-in/check-out data, progress notes, payroll and timesheet entries, incident reports, and expense claims.
  • Participant-related information — first name, last name, NDIS plan details, support goals, and service agreement data necessary to operate care workflow tools.
  • Authentication and session data — OAuth access tokens, refresh tokens, token metadata, IP addresses, and user-agent strings.
  • Audit and activity logs — records of tool calls, data accesses, and system events when you interact with the platform or ChatGPT connector.
  • Technical data — browser type, device identifiers, and connectivity metadata for security and performance purposes.

3. Sensitive Information

Some information we process is "sensitive information" under the Privacy Act 1988, including health information, disability information, and information about NDIS supports. We handle sensitive information with enhanced protections:

  • We collect sensitive information only when reasonably necessary for the direct purpose of operating your organisation's care services.
  • Access to sensitive information is restricted to users whose role and granted scopes permit it.
  • Sensitive information is never used for direct marketing, profiling, or any purpose outside the authorised operational scope.
  • Consent to process sensitive information is obtained as part of your organisation's service agreement with Plannor.

4. How We Use Personal Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

  • Providing the platform — delivering care coordination, rostering, billing, compliance, and reporting features your organisation has subscribed to.
  • Operating the AI connector — executing tool calls made through ChatGPT or other MCP-compatible AI platforms on behalf of the authenticated user, within their permitted scope.
  • Security and fraud prevention — verifying identity, detecting and preventing unauthorised access, abuse, or suspicious activity.
  • Audit and accountability — maintaining records of user actions for compliance, dispute resolution, and regulatory purposes.
  • Product improvement — analysing usage patterns in aggregate and de-identified form to improve platform reliability and features.
  • Legal obligations — complying with applicable laws, responding to regulatory enquiries, and enforcing our agreements.

5. Disclosure to Third Parties

We may disclose personal information to the following categories of recipients:

  • OpenAI (ChatGPT) — tool inputs and results are exchanged with OpenAI's infrastructure when you use the ChatGPT connector.
  • Google (Gemini AI) — the built-in Plannor chatbot sends conversation messages to Google's Gemini API for text generation.
  • Google (Calendar API) — shift information is transmitted to create, update, and delete calendar events. We request only the calendar.events scope and never access existing calendar content.
  • Xero — financial data including invoice details, payroll journal entries, and contact records are synchronised for accounting and payroll purposes.
  • Intuit (QuickBooks Online) — invoice records, contact information, payroll data, and payment records are synchronised with Intuit's API.
  • MYOB — invoice records, payroll data, contact information, and payment records are synchronised with MYOB's API.
  • Twilio (WhatsApp / SMS) — message content is transmitted via Twilio's API when notifications are enabled.
  • Cloud infrastructure providers — hosting, database, and storage services (currently hosted in Australia or adequate privacy regions).
  • Payment processors — Stripe and similar services that process billing and subscription information.
  • Regulatory bodies — government agencies or regulators (NDIS QSC, OAIC) when required by law.
We do not sell personal information to third parties.

6. Overseas Disclosure

Some personal information will be transferred to entities outside Australia when you use the following features:

  • ChatGPT connector & built-in AI chatbot — processed by OpenAI and Google (United States).
  • Google Calendar integration — shift event data processed by Google (United States).
  • Xero integration — financial and contact data processed by Xero (New Zealand / United States).
  • QuickBooks integration — financial and contact data processed by Intuit (United States).
  • MYOB integration — data processed by MYOB (Australia / New Zealand); some processing may occur overseas.
  • Twilio (WhatsApp / SMS) — message data processed by Twilio (United States).
Before disclosing information overseas, we take reasonable steps to ensure the overseas recipient handles it consistently with the Australian Privacy Principles. You consent to overseas disclosure when you activate and use these integrations.

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes it was collected or as required by law:

8. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal information against misuse, loss, unauthorised access, modification, or disclosure:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS/HTTPS) for all data exchanged with the platform and its connectors.
  • Server-side tenant isolation — your organisation's data is never accessible to another tenant.
  • Role-based access control — each user can only access tools and data their organisational role permits.
  • OAuth 2.1 with PKCE — the ChatGPT connector uses a secure authorisation flow that does not expose credentials.
  • Audit logging — all connector activity is logged and reviewable by your organisation's administrator.
No system is entirely secure. If you suspect a security incident, contact us immediately at privacy@plannor.com.au.

9. Your Rights — Access, Correction & Complaints

Under the Australian Privacy Principles you have the right to:

  • Access your personal information. Submit a request to privacy@plannor.com.au. We will respond within 30 days after verifying your identity.
  • Correct inaccurate information. If you believe information we hold is incorrect, notify us and we will correct it or note your dispute.
  • Make a privacy complaint. Lodge a complaint with us first — we acknowledge within 5 business days and respond within 30 days.
  • Escalate to the OAIC. If unsatisfied, contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner at www.oaic.gov.au or call 1300 363 992.

10. Data Breach Notification

In the event of an eligible data breach under the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme, we will:

  • Notify affected individuals and the OAIC as soon as practicable.
  • Provide a statement including the nature of the breach, kinds of information involved, and recommended steps for individuals.
  • Take immediate steps to contain the breach and prevent further unauthorised access.
  • Maintain an internal breach register and review security practices following any incident.

11. NDIS-Specific Obligations

Plannor is designed to support registered NDIS providers. When your organisation uses the platform to manage participant supports:

  • Your organisation (the NDIS provider) remains the data controller for participant information and is responsible for ensuring all use complies with the NDIS Act 2013, NDIS (Privacy and Confidentiality) information sheet, and NDIS Code of Conduct.
  • Participant information accessed through the connector must only be used for the purpose of delivering or coordinating supports.
  • Sharing participant information with AI platforms (ChatGPT, Gemini) via the connector should be limited to what is necessary and reviewed by your privacy officer before broad deployment.
  • The platform supports NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission incident reporting obligations by preserving incident records.

12. Cookies and Technical Tracking

The Plannor web application uses session cookies to maintain authenticated sessions. Legal pages and connector landing pages do not use advertising or analytics tracking cookies. If we add analytics in future, we will update this policy and obtain any required consent.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. Material changes will be communicated by publishing the updated policy at this URL and, where appropriate, notifying affected users by email. Continued use of the platform after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised terms.
